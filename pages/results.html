Results

All results are on simulated truth: msprime admixture scenarios with a census event placed just older than the admixture pulse, so every lineage is unambiguously in a source population and the true local ancestry is known per haplotype (simulate_admixture, local_ancestry_truth). Figures are embedded from the notebooks — the code that produced them is one click away.

Accuracy & calibration

tspaint’s distinguishing value is a calibrated soft posterior, not raw accuracy. Predicted P(A) tracks the empirical frequency (points near the diagonal):

Source: Calibration & accuracy vs admixture age

Discrimination is strong at recent-to-moderate admixture and decays at old admixture — but not from tree-inference error: under present-day sampling, admixed lineages coalesce among themselves before an old pulse, severing the query→reference genealogical link. This is a coalescent limit; the crossover age scales with \(N_e\) and the sampling scheme.

Fragmentation & tract-length fidelity

Downstream admixture-pulse dating reads the segment-length distribution, so spurious short opposite-ancestry calls bias the inferred pulse older. Naive argmax of the posterior over-fragments; because the posterior is calibrated, a modest confidence deadband (paint(deadband=…) / hard_segments) suppresses the low-confidence flips and recovers the true switch density:

Source: Fragmentation & tract-length fidelity

so the inferred tract-length distribution matches the truth:

Over 6 seeds (true ARG, strong structure, T_admix=200, 5 Mb), the switch-density ratio (inferred / true) is 1.95 ± 0.58 for naive argmax but 0.99 ± 0.01 with a deadband of 0.4 — recovering the true distribution near-perfectly, and beating RFMix’s native segmentation (1.35 ± 0.22).

Head-to-head vs. RFMix

Against the field-standard segment incumbent (RFMix v2; wired as rfmix_paint), tspaint matches balanced accuracy across regimes while staying calibrated where RFMix saturates to 0/1:

regime rfmix tspaint nearest-ref baseline
strong structure (Ne 1e3, split 5e3) 0.99 / conf 1.00 0.99 / conf 0.68 0.99 / conf 1.00
weak structure (Ne 1e4, split 2e3) 0.78–0.85 / conf 0.92–1.0 0.77–0.80 / conf 0.10 0.79–0.82 / conf 1.0

(balanced accuracy / mean confidence). Neither dominates on accuracy; tspaint’s edge is the calibrated soft posterior + readable Q / credibility.

Inferred ARGs & the horizontal BP smoother

Admixture dating: rate through time

A separate deliverable (see admixture dating): the time-inhomogeneous estimator fits the directional cross-ancestry rates q_AB(t), q_BA(t). On a clean split the rate is ~0 more recently than the divergence and rises across it — here the inferred onset lands within ~1 % of the true split time:

Source: Admixture dating: cross-ancestry rate through time

Across the validation rungs (true ARG) it sharpens the onset toward the true split, resolves a pulse / asymmetric pulse / ongoing migration, and survives on inferred (SINGER) ARGs — where the binding axis is calibration rather than point accuracy. Feeding Q(t) back into the painting is not on the default path: it adds no painting accuracy, only sharper (possibly mis-calibrated) confidence at ~4–5× cost, so painting and dating stay side by side.

Reproducing

Every figure above is computed in a notebook; the end-to-end drivers live in tspaint.experiments (e.g. age_sweep, fragmentation_experiment, bp_vs_deadband_experiment), with the dating estimator in tspaint.dating.
